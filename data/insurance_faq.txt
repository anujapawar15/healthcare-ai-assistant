INSURANCE ELIGIBILITY FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
Springfield General Hospital - Billing and Insurance Department

What insurance plans does Springfield General Hospital accept?
Springfield General Hospital accepts most major insurance plans including Medicare, Medicaid, Blue Cross Blue Shield, Aetna, Cigna, United Healthcare, Humana, and many other regional and local plans. Call the billing department at 555-000-3000 to confirm whether your specific plan is accepted before scheduling.

How can I verify my insurance eligibility before my appointment?
Patients can verify insurance coverage by contacting their insurance company directly using the number on the insurance card, logging into the patient portal and entering insurance information for real-time eligibility verification, or calling the billing department at 555-000-3000 at least 48 hours before the appointment.

What is a co-pay and when is it due?
A co-pay is a fixed amount you pay for a covered healthcare service at the time of the visit. Co-pay amounts vary depending on your insurance plan. Co-pays are collected at check-in and must be paid before services are rendered.

What is prior authorization and when is it needed?
Prior authorization is approval from your insurance company before certain services, procedures, or medications are covered. Services that commonly require prior authorization include elective surgeries, advanced imaging such as MRI or CT scans, specialist referrals, and certain prescription medications. Our staff will assist in obtaining prior authorization, but the process may take 3 to 10 business days.

What happens if my insurance claim is denied?
If a claim is denied, our billing team will review the denial reason and assist with the appeals process. Common reasons for denial include services not covered under the plan, incorrect billing codes, and lack of prior authorization. Patients will receive a written explanation of benefits from their insurer detailing the reason for denial.

Do you offer financial assistance for uninsured patients?
Yes. Springfield General Hospital offers a Charity Care Program for patients who are uninsured or underinsured. Financial counselors are available Monday through Friday from 9 AM to 4 PM to assess eligibility. Applications for financial assistance must be submitted within 90 days of the service date.

What is a deductible?
A deductible is the amount you must pay out-of-pocket before your insurance begins to cover services. For example if your deductible is $1,000 you pay the first $1,000 of covered healthcare costs each year before insurance pays. After meeting your deductible you typically pay coinsurance until you reach your out-of-pocket maximum.

For billing questions contact 555-000-3000 or billing@springfieldgeneral.org
