PATIENT DISCHARGE INSTRUCTIONS
Springfield General Hospital

Activity and Rest
Rest is essential during the first 24 to 48 hours after discharge. Avoid strenuous physical activity, heavy lifting over 10 pounds, or vigorous exercise for at least one week unless your physician specifies otherwise. Walking is encouraged as tolerated, starting with short distances and gradually increasing. Do not drive or operate heavy machinery for 24 hours after receiving anesthesia or sedation medications. Return to work should be discussed with your treating physician based on the nature of your condition and job requirements.

Wound and Incision Care
Keep the wound or incision site clean and dry. Change dressings as instructed by your nurse before discharge. Avoid submerging the wound in water such as swimming or taking baths until cleared by your physician. Monitor the wound for signs of infection including increased redness, warmth, swelling, discharge with an unpleasant odor, or fever above 100.4 degrees Fahrenheit. Do not remove staples, sutures, or surgical strips unless directed by your healthcare provider.

Medications
Take all prescribed medications as directed. Do not skip doses or stop medications without consulting your doctor. Over-the-counter pain relievers such as acetaminophen may be used as directed unless contraindicated. Avoid alcohol while taking prescription pain medications or antibiotics. If you experience severe side effects such as difficulty breathing, rash, or swelling, seek emergency care immediately.

Diet and Nutrition
Follow any dietary restrictions specified by your care team. Stay well-hydrated by drinking 6 to 8 glasses of water per day unless restricted. Eat light, easily digestible meals for the first 24 to 48 hours. Avoid fried or spicy foods during initial recovery.

Follow-Up Appointments
Schedule a follow-up appointment with your primary care provider within 7 to 14 days of discharge. Contact our scheduling department at 555-000-1234 to book your follow-up visit. Bring all discharge paperwork and a list of your current medications to your follow-up appointment.

Emergency Warning Signs
Seek immediate emergency care if you experience chest pain or pressure, difficulty breathing or shortness of breath, high fever above 101.5 degrees Fahrenheit, severe pain not controlled by medication, sudden confusion or difficulty speaking, bleeding that does not stop with pressure, or signs of blood clot such as sudden swelling, redness, or pain in the leg.
