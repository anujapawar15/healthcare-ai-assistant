MEDICATION REFILL POLICY
Springfield General Hospital - Pharmacy and Clinical Services

General Refill Guidelines
Springfield General Hospital processes medication refill requests according to established clinical protocols to ensure patient safety and appropriate medication management. Patients are encouraged to request refills before their medication runs out to allow sufficient processing time.

How to Request a Medication Refill
Patients may request refills through the patient portal by submitting a request online at least 5 business days before medication runs out, by calling the pharmacy line at 555-000-5000 Monday through Friday 9 AM to 5 PM, during a scheduled telehealth consultation for certain non-controlled medications, or during an in-person visit with the healthcare provider.

Processing Time
Routine refill requests are typically processed within 2 to 3 business days. Requests submitted on weekends or holidays will be processed the next business day. Allow additional time for prior authorization requirements from insurance.

Controlled Substance Refill Policy
Controlled substances including Schedule II through V medications are subject to stricter federal and state regulations. Schedule II medications such as certain opioids and stimulants cannot be refilled electronically and require a new written or electronic prescription each time. Controlled substance refills will not be processed early. Early refill requests will be denied unless there is documented evidence of loss or theft which must be reported to local law enforcement. Telehealth prescribing of controlled substances is subject to DEA regulations and may require an in-person evaluation.

Chronic Medication Refills
Patients on long-term medications such as blood pressure medications, diabetes medications, or thyroid medications may receive refills for up to a 90-day supply with provider authorization. Patients on chronic medications should schedule annual wellness visits to ensure continued appropriateness of their prescriptions.

Expired Prescriptions
Prescriptions that have exceeded 12 months from the original issue date cannot be refilled. A new evaluation and prescription from a provider will be required before a refill can be processed.

Prior Authorization for Refills
Some medications require prior authorization from the insurance provider for refills. Hospital staff will initiate the prior authorization process but this may delay refills by 3 to 10 business days.

Refill Denials
Refill requests may be denied if the patient has not had a required follow-up visit, the medication requires a new evaluation, a drug-drug interaction or safety concern has been identified, or the prescription has expired. Patients who have their refill denied will receive notification with the reason for denial.

Medication Refills Through Telehealth
Medication refill requests may be reviewed during telehealth visits for certain non-controlled medications. Patients requesting refills during a telehealth visit must have an active valid prescription and must not have had a change in symptoms or health status that would require in-person evaluation.

For medication questions contact the pharmacy line at 555-000-5000.
